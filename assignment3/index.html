GET TO KNOW THESE CUTE ANIMALS!

MOO DENG

Moo Deng is a pygmy hippopotamus born on July 10, 2024, at the Khao Kheow Open Zoo in Thailand. She quickly became an internet sensation due to her adorable and startled expressions, especially a viral video where she looks terrified while being held by her caretaker. Her playful, bouncy nature earned her the nickname "Moo Deng," which means "Bouncy Pork" in Thai.

As a pygmy hippo, Moo Deng belongs to a smaller, rarer species of hippopotamus native to the forests and swamps of West Africa, primarily in Liberia, Sierra Leone, Guinea, and Ivory Coast. Unlike the common hippopotamus, pygmy hippos are more reclusive and spend much of their time in dense forests near water sources. They are much smaller, typically weighing between 180 to 275 kg, compared to their larger cousins, which can weigh over 1,500 kg.

Pygmy hippos are endangered, with an estimated population of fewer than 2,500 individuals in the wild. Conservation efforts are critical for their survival due to habitat destruction and hunting in their natural range.

PESTO

Pesto the Penguin is a baby king penguin who lives at the SEA LIFE Melbourne Aquarium in Australia. Born in 2023, Pesto quickly gained fame due to his adorable, fluffy appearance and charming, clumsy waddle. At just nine months old, Pesto towers over his parents, weighing an impressive 21 kg.

His fame was sparked by videos and photos showcasing his cute waddling and playful interactions with his surroundings. Fans online have been captivated by his fluffy down feathers and lovable demeanor, which have made Pesto one of the aquarium's star attractions.

King penguins like Pesto are the second-largest penguin species in the world and are known for their distinctive yellow-orange markings on their heads and necks. They typically inhabit sub-Antarctic islands, where they form large colonies and thrive in cold environments.

MOLÉ

Molé is an orphaned, two-toed sloth currently residing at the wildlife rescue center in Costa Rica. After losingg his mother, Molé formed a strong attachment to a stuffed teddy bear, which provides him with comfort and acts as a surrogate. The rescue team is working on rehabilitating Molé with enrichment activities in hopes of eventually releasing him back into the wild. Molé has become a viral sensation, capturing the hearts of millions with his heartwarming story and adorable appearance.

Sloths like Molé are known for their slow movements and distinctive features, such as long limbs and a sleepy demeanor. They typically live in tropical forests and spend most of their lives hanging upside down in trees. Toe-twed sloths are noctornal and primarily eat leaves, fruits, and flowers. Conservation efforts are crucial for sloths, as habitat destructionand poaching threaten their population.

BISCUITS

Biscuits is an ultra-adorable rescued seal pup currently under the care of the Vancouver Marine Mammal Rescue Society (VAMMR) in Canada. She gained widespread attention through a viral video showcasing her upright pose and sweet, wide-eyed expression. Her big, black eyes and curious demeanor have endeared her to viewers.

In the video, VAMMR asked people to add Biscuits to their list of favorite baby animals, which has helped raise awareness for the organisation's work in rescuing and rehabilitating marine mammals. Biscuits' charm has significantly boosted her visibility online, making her a star in the animal rescue community.

Seals like Biscuits are commonly found along the coasts and spend much of their time in the water, relying on fish and other marine life for food. Due to threats like pollution, entanglement in fishing gear, and habitat loss, many seals require rescue and rehabilitation before they can be returned to the wild.

HUA HUA

Hua Hua is a female giant panda at the Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding in China, who became famous for her distinct look and gentle demeanor. Born in 2020, Hua Hua has a unique appearance, with upside-down, teardrop-shaped black eye patches. She also has some developmental delays, making her smaller and slower compared to other pandas of her age.

Despite these challenges, Hua Hua has won the hearts of many with her calm, unfazed attitude, especially in viral videos where she patiently tolerates her food being stolen by bigger peers. Her charming demeanor has garnered her a significant fan base, with over 14.7 billion views on the Chinese social media platform Weibo.

Her story highlights the importance of panda conservation, as Hua Hua, like other giant pandas, is part of a breeding program aimed at preserving the species, which is still considered vulnerable due to habitat loss and other factors.

JABULANI

Jabulani is a Southern white rhino who was bon on August 18, 2024 at Werribee Open Range Zoo in Australia. His name means "rejoice" in Zulu. The public voted for this name, which reflects the joy he brought to the zoo, his family, and all who followed his journey.

Despite his young age, Jabulani quickly developed a bold and playful personality, even engaging in mock charges, a behavior typical for rhinos as they learn to assert themselves.

Southern white rhinos, native to Southern Africa, are currently classified as Near Threatened, with only around 10,080 individuals remaining in the wild due to poaching and habitat loss. Jabulani is part of a broader conservation effort through the Australasian Southern White Rhino Breeding Program, which aims to protect the species from further decline.
